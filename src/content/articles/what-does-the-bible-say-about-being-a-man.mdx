The Bible does not define manhood by muscle, money, or status. It defines it by responsibility, courage, and the willingness to serve at personal cost. If you are a young man wondering what Scripture says about who you are supposed to become, the answer is more demanding — and more freeing — than culture suggests.

## The Pattern Is Service, Not Status

When the disciples argued about who was the greatest, Jesus answered with a reversal that still cuts: "Whoever wants to become great among you must be your servant" (Mark 10:43).

This was not metaphor. He demonstrated it by washing their feet the night before he died. The King kneeling with a towel. That is the template.

## What Scripture Actually Asks of You

### Courage Without Aggression

"Be on your guard; stand firm in the faith; be courageous; be strong. Do everything in love" (1 Corinthians 16:13-14). Notice the structure — strength and love in the same breath. Not one or the other. Both.

### Responsibility Before Recognition

"Whatever you do, work at it with all your heart, as working for the Lord, not for human masters" (Colossians 3:23). A man who needs an audience to do his best work has not yet understood the assignment.

### Self-Control as Strength

Proverbs 25:28: "Like a city whose walls are broken through is a person who lacks self-control." In the ancient world, a city without walls was defenseless. A man without discipline is the same.

## The Men Scripture Remembers

David was remembered not for killing Goliath but for being "a man after God's own heart" (Acts 13:22). Joseph was remembered for his integrity in Potiphar's house — the quiet refusal when no one was watching. Daniel was remembered for praying with his windows open when it could have killed him.

None of these men were perfect. All of them were faithful.

## What This Means For You

Being a man, according to Scripture, is not a destination. It is a daily practice:

- **Serve** before you are asked.
- **Speak truth** when it costs you something.
- **Protect** those who cannot protect themselves.
- **Build** things that will outlast you.
- **Pray** as if the outcome depends on God, and work as if it depends on you.
