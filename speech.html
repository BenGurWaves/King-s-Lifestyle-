Building a Kingly Vocabulary

A king does not use ten words when five will suffice, nor five when one precise word says everything. Expand your vocabulary not to impress, but to express with surgical precision.

Replace These Immediately

Nice → Admirable, refined, commendable
Very good → Exceptional, outstanding, exemplary
Bad → Inadequate, deplorable, substandard
I think → I believe, I'm convinced, in my assessment
Stuff → Matters, particulars, elements
Like, um, uh → [Silence] — the king's most powerful word

The Filler-Word Eliminator

"Um," "like," "you know," "basically" — these are the cracks in a king's armor. They signal uncertainty, lack of preparation, or carelessness. Replace every filler with a deliberate pause. Silence is authority.

The 7-Day Filler Detox

Day 1-2: Record yourself speaking for 2 minutes. Count every filler. This is your baseline.

Day 3-4: Practice speaking in 30-second bursts with zero fillers. Pause instead.

Day 5-6: Have a full conversation while consciously replacing fillers with silence.

Day 7: Record again. Compare to Day 1. You will be astonished.
