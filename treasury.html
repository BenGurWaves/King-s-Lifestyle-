The King's Budget Framework

A king does not count pennies — he allocates resources with strategic precision. The Royal Budget divides every coin with purpose:

50% — The Kingdom's Needs

Housing, food, transport, insurance. Non-negotiable. These keep the kingdom running.

20% — The War Chest

Savings, emergency fund (6 months minimum), debt elimination. This is your financial armor.

20% — The Investment Vault

Index funds, retirement accounts, real estate, education. Money that works while you sleep builds dynasties.

10% — The King's Pleasure & Giving

Generosity first. Then measured enjoyment. A king who gives freely is trusted; one who hoards is feared.

The Seven Laws of Wealth Building

I. Pay yourself first. Before any bill, before any want — the treasury gets filled.

II. Eliminate consumer debt with the ferocity of a king defending his borders.

III. Invest consistently, not cleverly. Time in the market defeats timing the market.

IV. Build multiple income streams. A kingdom with one road is easily sieged.

V. Protect your assets. Insurance, estate planning, emergency funds — the wall before the storm.

VI. Teach your children. Generational wealth is not inherited — it is educated into existence.

VII. Give generously. Wealth hoarded rots; wealth shared multiplies and builds your name.
