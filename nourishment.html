LESSON 02

Clean vs Unclean — The Design Rules Every King Must Know

Two tests for land animals: does it split the hoof, and does it chew the cud? Both must be true. The cow passes. The deer passes. The pig splits the hoof but does not chew the cud — it fails. No ambiguity.

For sea creatures: fins and scales. Both. The salmon passes. The shrimp has neither — it fails. These bottom-feeders are the ocean's garbage processors. They were designed to clean the water, not your bloodstream.

"Whatever parts the hoof and is cloven-footed and chews the cud, among the animals, you may eat."

— Leviticus 11:3

"Nature designed filtration systems and consumption systems. The wise man does not eat the filter."

— Natural Philosophy

LAND

Hoof + Cud = Clean

Cattle, sheep, goat, deer, bison, elk. Multi-chamber stomachs that filter toxins before the meat reaches you.

SEA

Fins + Scales = Clean

Salmon, cod, trout, bass, sardine, herring. Scales act as a biological barrier against toxin absorption.

AIR

Foragers, Not Scavengers

Chicken, turkey, quail, dove. Avoid raptors and scavengers. Clean birds eat grain and seed, not carrion.

Practical Steps

LESSON 16

Butchering Basics & Meat Preparation

Know the cuts: rib-eye for grilling, chuck for slow cooking, sirloin for versatility, tenderloin for occasions. Season 30 minutes before cooking. Sear on high, finish on low, rest 5+ minutes. Cutting too early loses all the juice. Patience is a cooking skill.

"Everything that lives and moves about will be food for you."

— Genesis 9:3

"Understanding where food comes from is the first step toward respecting what it becomes."

— Culinary Foundation

CUTS

Know Your Beef

Rib-eye: rich. Chuck: flavorful, slow cook. Sirloin: lean. Tenderloin: special occasions.

METHOD

Sear and Rest

High heat for crust. Low heat for center. Rest 5 minutes minimum. Patience saves the meal.

STORAGE

Fresh vs Frozen

Fresh within 3 days. Freeze airtight up to 6 months. Thaw in refrigerator, never on counter.

Practical Steps
