The King's Table: Clean Eating

A king does not eat to fill — he eats to fuel. Every meal is a decision: will this strengthen my reign or weaken it? Clean eating is not deprivation; it is the deliberate choice to give your body what it was designed to receive.

The Royal Plate Method

  • Half the plate: Vegetables and greens — the foundation of vitality
  • Quarter plate: Lean protein — the builder of strength
  • Quarter plate: Complex carbohydrates — the sustainer of energy
  • A measure of healthy fats: Olive oil, avocado, nuts — the protector of mind

Foods a King Avoids

Processed sugars, excessive alcohol, anything that clouds judgment or saps energy. A king's clarity of thought begins at his table.

Gentleman Dining Etiquette

How you eat reveals who you are. Master these protocols and you'll command respect at any table — from a Michelin-starred restaurant to a family Sunday dinner.

The Napkin

Unfold on your lap within 30 seconds of sitting. Never tuck it into your collar. When leaving temporarily, place it on the chair.

Utensil Mastery

Continental style: fork in left, knife in right. Never switch. Rest utensils at 4:20 position when pausing, parallel at 6:30 when finished.

Ordering

Let others order first. Never the cheapest or most expensive item. Ask the waiter for recommendations — it shows confidence, not ignorance.

Conversation at Table

Never speak with food in your mouth. Chew slowly. Make eye contact. The dinner table is where empires are built through relationship.
