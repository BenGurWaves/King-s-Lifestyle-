The King's Physical Foundation

A king's training is not about aesthetics alone — it is about functional sovereignty. The ability to protect, to endure, to carry weight (literal and figurative), and to command physical presence in any room.

The Four Pillars of Royal Fitness

Strength

Compound movements: deadlift, squat, bench press, overhead press. These build the frame of a man who is taken seriously. 3-4 sessions per week.

Endurance

Cardiovascular health ensures longevity of rule. Brisk walking, swimming, cycling. 150 minutes per week minimum. A king who tires easily loses wars.

Mobility

Flexibility and joint health. Daily stretching, yoga, or mobility work. A stiff king moves like a man afraid. A limber king moves like one who owns the room.

Recovery

Sleep 7-9 hours. Cold exposure. Adequate hydration. A king who runs himself into the ground serves no one. Recovery is not weakness — it is strategy.
