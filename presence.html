The King's Walk

Your walk is your opening statement. Before you speak, before you shake hands, you walk — and everyone watches.

The Protocol

  • Pace: 10-15% slower than your natural speed. Urgency signals anxiety. Measured pace signals authority.
  • Stride: Shoulder-width. Not too wide (aggressive), not too narrow (timid).
  • Arms: Relaxed at sides with gentle natural swing. Never in pockets. Never crossed behind the back (military, not regal).
  • Head: Level, chin parallel to the ground. Eyes forward, not at the floor. You are surveying your domain.
  • Shoulders: Pulled back and down. Not tense, not slouched. Imagine wearing a heavy crown — it straightens everything.

The Art of Sitting

How a man sits reveals his inner state. The sloucher is defeated. The fidgeter is anxious. The king sits with grounded stillness.

Boardroom

Upright, both feet flat, hands on table or armrests. Lean slightly forward when listening — it communicates engagement and authority.

Social Setting

Relaxed but controlled. One ankle may rest on opposite knee. Arms open. Take up appropriate space — a king does not shrink himself.

Dining

Straight back, wrists resting on table edge (never elbows). Lean from the waist when eating, not from the neck. Grace in every gesture.

Body Language in Every Setting

The Handshake

Firm (not crushing), web-to-web contact, 2-3 pumps, direct eye contact, slight smile. This 3-second interaction forms 80% of a first impression. Practice until it's second nature.

Eye Contact

The triangle method: alternate gaze between left eye, right eye, and bridge of nose. Hold for 3-5 seconds, then briefly look away. Never stare. Never dart. Steady, warm, confident.

Standing in a Group

Weight evenly distributed, feet shoulder-width, hands visible (never hidden in pockets for long). Position yourself to include everyone. The king opens circles, never closes them.

Absurd Fix: The Pool Party

You're at a pool party in swim trunks — no suit to hide behind. Your presence comes from: posture (stand tall), towel draped on one shoulder (not wrapped around you), confident casual movement. Don't rush in or out of the water. Enter slowly. Own the space.
